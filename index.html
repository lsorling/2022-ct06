Piet Mondrian

One day I was out shopping and I was attracted to this marketing poster of a hair salon using color blocking. I was curious about it, and I went back and start researching. It didn't take me long to find this guy named "Piet Mondrian".

Color Blocking

Color blocking has been around for around 10 decades. Piet Mondrian was the first artist to really popularize the idea of color blocking. Piet Mondrian introduced color blocking in the 1930's by creating works using vibrant colors separated by thick black lines making squares of different colors, most commonly, blue, red, yellow, the primary colors; and white, and black. This new vision for modern art had profound effects on the artistic world, as well as, the fashion world.
